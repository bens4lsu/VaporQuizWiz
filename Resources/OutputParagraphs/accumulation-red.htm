Accumulation Mindset

Executing your plan to achieve your goals.

Your Distance From Your Goal:

Your answers on this mindset indicate that you do need to take action. But don’t worry, there’s no need to panic. You can take simple steps to move toward having what you really want in your life.

Where should you start? By clarifying your goals.

Do you want to take a fabulous trip, save for your child’s education, or just feel more financially secure? Gaining clarity on what you want to achieve and why you want to achieve it can prove motivating.

Don’t forget the “why,” because when you begin to veer off track (which you likely will), the “why” pulls you back to center, to what you really want.

Key Action Steps

Next, take these simple but powerful steps to “free up” money to save for your goals:

  • Begin by gathering your credit card statements, bank statements (including debit card statements), and pay stubs for the previous 90 days.

  • Use this information to calculate an average monthly expenditure over the last 90 days.

  • Now, let’s “drill down” a bit by looking at individual expenses during these 90 days. Flag each one as either “discretionary” or “essential.” Look particularly for “easy wins,” such as monthly subscriptions you no longer need or entertainment expenses that may be larger than you realize. 

  • Prioritize your spending by identifying expenses that you can either decrease or eliminate.

  • Now that you’ve increased your cash flow, put this money aside monthly—ideally, deposit it automatically in a separate account that you earmark for this goal.

These simple steps create a virtually automatic system that will move you more rapidly toward what you really want in your life.
