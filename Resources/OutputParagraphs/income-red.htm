On this mindset, your answers reveal a need to take action, to ensure that you can enjoy the kind of “next chapter” you want. But there’s not need to panic: We’ll outline steps you can take right now to help you move more rapidly toward the life you want.

Key Action Steps

  • First, reassess your goals, to ensure that you know what you want your “next chapter” to look like.
  • Review the section in "Your Walkaway Passport®" report on planning for the next chapter and take any steps that we suggest there, to make sure that you have a clear picture of where you’re headed and a concrete plan for getting there.
  • Once you’ve articulated a clear vision, the next step is to quantify the assets that you have available to produce a retirement income stream. These assets can include investments, retirement funds, pensions, savings, and social security.
  • Next, estimate your monthly income needs in retirement, ensuring that you consider possible inflation, medical costs, living expenses, and discretionary spending. Doing so can be daunting—often, this provides a good reason to work with a professional financial planner to ensure you know your real number. Don’t forget: You should maintain a separate emergency fund, even in retirement. Estimating “what you’ll have” and “what you’ll need” in this way can illuminate gaps in your income plan and opportunities for improvement.
  • Consider consulting a financial advisor to help you clarify your goals further and plan to achieve them. The concluding section of this report provides relevant information. 

These steps can help ensure you can enjoy the "walkaway" that you really want.
