Your answers indicate that you’ve done a good job of establishing the habit of tracking your income and your expenses. That’s a critically important step in securing your financial future. 

Key Action Steps

We suggest you now take some simple additional steps that can help you free up cash flow:

  • Begin by reassessing your goals: For what do you really want to use your money?

  • Form a monthly habit of tracking your expenses, to ensure that you’re on the right road. We suggest you schedule a recurring event in your calendar each month that designates the date and time that you will review that month’s income and expenses.

  • Gather your pay stubs, credit card statements, and bank statements (including debit card statements) for that month.

  • Look closely at individual expenditures, flagging each one as either “discretionary” or “essential.” Look particularly for “easy wins,” such as subscriptions you no longer need or entertainment expenses that may be larger than you realize. 

  • Prioritize your spending by identifying expenses that you can either decrease or eliminate.

  • This may also be a good time to look at your current career and/or income to see if there are ways to earn additional income (such as asking for a raise, looking at a different opportunity, or adding another form of income).

These simple steps will help you free cash flow to fund the things you really want, your goals.
