Congratulations on making progress toward ensuring that you can afford long-term care if it becomes necessary. Now it's time to take the next steps.

Key Action Steps

We suggest that you begin by reassessing your goals. What kind of long-term care would you prefer?

Be sure that you articulate goals for different health scenarios. Do you want to

  • Stay at home at all costs?
  • Live with a family member?
  • Have outside assistance or help from a loved one?
  • Be in a vibrant retirement community or only move if and when care is needed?

Although few of us want to talk about these questions, they prove critically important to address.

Next, it’s time to (a) calculate your need, (b) define your risk, and potentially (c) transfer your risk.

  • Calculating the need means coming up with a number that represents the likely cost of long-term care. Be sure that you know the cost of care in your state and/or local area.
  • Calculating the risk means measuring the difference between your total needs and the savings and protections (insurance) that you currently have in place.
  • To do that, use an insurance calculator or speak with a financial advisor. To transfer your risk, consider putting long-term care insurance in place or augmenting your current coverage.
  • In addition to measuring your need and risk, don’t forget to also ensure that you have wills, trusts, powers of attorney, etc. in place to protect the ones you love the most. 

Taking these steps now will provide peace of mind for you and your loved ones.
