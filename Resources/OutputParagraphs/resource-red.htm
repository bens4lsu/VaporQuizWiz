Your Distance From Your Goal:

Your answers reveal an opportunity to improve your financial situation by freeing up cash flow. Remember: It’s not just how much money you make that determines your financial situation but also how much money you keep.

Often, we miss opportunities to increase cash flow because we’re unaware of how much we’re spending. That’s why it’s a good idea to track your spending so that you become keenly aware of how much money flows through your hands.

Key Action Steps

The good news is that you can take simple steps, right now, to identify where and how to increase cash flow:

  • Begin by gathering your credit card statements, bank statements (including debit card statements), and pay stubs for the previous 90 days.

  • Use this information to calculate an average monthly expenditure over the last 90 days.

  • Now, let’s “drill down” a bit by looking at individual expenses during these 90 days. Flag each one as either “discretionary” or “essential.” Look particularly for “easy wins,” such as subscriptions you no longer need or entertainment expenses that may be larger than you realize. 

  • Prioritize your spending by identifying expenses that you can either decrease or eliminate.

  • Monitor your expenses monthly, continuing to identify opportunities to increase your cash flow.

  • This may also be a good time to look at your current career and/or income to see if there are ways to earn additional income (such as asking for a raise, looking at a different opportunity, or adding another form of income).

These simple steps can free up cash so that you can save for things you really want, achieve your goals more rapidly, and decrease stress in the process.
