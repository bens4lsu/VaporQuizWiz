Wellness Mindset

Enhancing your health and wellness so you can enjoy your life and live it fully.

Your Distance From Your Goal:

Although investing in yourself may seem optional, doing so can yield dividends not only in the way you feel but also in your personal, business, and financial life.

Key Action Steps

The good news is that you can make rapid progress if you focus your efforts:

  • Begin by clarifying your goals for this mindset—what’s truly most important to you? Do you want to increase the odds that you can live and work healthily beyond conventional expectations? Do you want to maintain a healthy weight, enjoy more energy, or be fit enough to enjoy particular activities? The first step is to answer these questions so that you understand why you want to focus on health and fitness. 
  • Next, find a way to track your progress toward your goals. Let’s start with healthy eating. Assess your eating habits, to see if they’re consistent with your health goals. If they aren’t, look for an eating plan that fits your goals and preferences. Consider tracking what you eat.
  • Then do the same with your activity—track your progress, whether with a fitness tracker, journal, or something else. Consider incorporating apps, devices, and personal trainers to make it fun to exercise and eat healthily.
  • And make your goals realistic. We suggest beginning with moderate exercise three times a week and a goal to eat healthily 4-5 days per week. Focus on making healthy eating and exercise a part of who you truly are.
  • Don't forget that wellness includes a mental as well as a physical component. Think about whether you need to manage stress more effectively--particularly if your family, career, or business places high demands on you. The American Institute of Stress states that over 80% of workers report experiencing work-related stress. If you're one of them, consider incorporating mindfulness and/or meditation techniques (many apps are available). Ensure that you seek support from friends and/or family and focus on positive coping strategies.

These simple actions can put you on a path to better wellness.
