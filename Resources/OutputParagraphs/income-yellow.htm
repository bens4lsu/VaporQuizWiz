You’ve made good progress on your “walkaway” income plan. Now it’s time to take the next steps.

Key Action Steps

  • First, make sure that you hold a clear vision of what you want your “next chapter” to look like. 
  • Review the section in "Your Walkaway Passport®" report on planning for the next chapter. Take any steps that we suggest there, to ensure you have a clear picture of where you’re headed and a concrete plan for getting there.
  • Once you’ve ensured that your vision is clear, the next step is to quantify the assets you have available to produce a retirement income stream. These assets can include investments, retirement funds, pensions, savings, and social security.
  • Then you should estimate your monthly income needs in retirement, ensuring that you consider possible inflation, medical costs, living expenses, and discretionary spending. Doing so can be daunting—often, this provides a good reason to work with a professional financial planner, to ensure you know your real number. Don’t forget: You should maintain a separate emergency fund, even in retirement. Estimating “what you’ll have” and “what you’ll need” in this way can illuminate gaps in your income plan and opportunities for improvement.
  • Consider consulting a financial advisor to help you clarify your goals further and plan to achieve them. The concluding section of this report provides relevant information.

These steps can help ensure you can enjoy the "walkaway" you really want.
